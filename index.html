Our story

We started VoidForge Systems
because building a PC shouldn't be a hassle.

Buy from the big-box brands and you get a machine stuffed with bloatware, locked-down parts, and software you never asked for. And if you actually want a custom build? Good luck. There's nowhere to just configure one online — you're stuck on a phone call for hours, spelling out every part you want while someone upsells you on the rest.

We thought that was backwards. You should be able to build exactly the rig you want, right on the website, in minutes. Pick your parts, see your price, place your order — no sales calls, no bloatware, no mystery markup. Just the machine you actually wanted.

So we built it. Our configurator does in a few clicks what used to take a whole afternoon on the phone. Behind it are two people, one workbench, and standards we won't compromise on: hand assembly, a 2-hour burn-in, brand-new authentic parts, and real human support — and if anything's ever wrong, you contact us and we make it right.

No bloat. No phone tag. Just a clean, custom machine built the way you spec'd it. We're shipping our first builds now — reserve one of the remaining founding slots and we'd love to earn your trust.

Common questions

Honest answers.

Most of what new customers want to know before submitting a build request.

You're a new shop. Why should I trust you with a $3,000 build?
Fair question. Here's the honest answer: trust isn't something a new shop can claim — we have to earn it. Three things we do differently to make that less of a leap for you:

One, you don't pay anything until we send you a confirmed quote with parts in stock. We can't take your money on a build we can't actually source.

Two, every build is fully stress-tested before it ships. If something is going to fail, it fails on our bench — not on your desk. We don't ship a machine that isn't working.

Three, if anything's ever wrong with your rig, you don't argue with NVIDIA or Newegg — you email us, and we help make it right.
How long does a build actually take?
Standard tiers (Pulsar, Nova, Quasar): 7–14 business days from quote approval to the day your rig leaves our shop. Add 3–5 days for ground shipping depending on where you are.

Custom or halo-tier builds: 3–4 weeks. Custom paint, special-order parts, and bespoke wiring take real time. We'd rather give you an honest timeline than promise 48 hours and burn out.

Every build is a priority build — we don't make you pay extra to skip a line.
Where do you source parts from?
Every build uses brand-new, authentic components sourced from authorized US retailers and major brand distributors. The parts are new and genuine, so each one keeps the manufacturer warranty it normally carries.

We don't use gray-market parts, refurbished components without disclosure, or unauthorized resellers. Every part on your build sheet is exactly what's specified — no substitutions.
What if something goes wrong after I get the rig?
Every machine is fully tested before it ships, so it arrives working. Your parts are brand-new and carry their own manufacturer warranties. If anything ever goes wrong with your rig, email us — we'll help you figure it out and make it right.
Can I supply my own parts?
We don't do customer-supplied parts on first-time builds. We can't stand behind components we didn't inspect or source ourselves.

We're happy to spec around any preference you have — favorite GPU model, specific case, particular RGB ecosystem. Send those preferences with your build request.
Do you ship outside the US?
Not currently. We ship within the continental United States only. International shipping is on the roadmap once we have a stable domestic operation; we'd rather do one thing well than two things badly.
What's the difference between Pulsar, Nova, and Quasar?
Pulsar E5 (from $2,599) — Built for high-refresh competitive gaming. Lean on CPU and GPU, low-latency tuned, smaller chassis.

Nova C7 (from $3,999) — Creator workstation. More cores, more RAM, dual NVMe for project files, tuned for thermal stability under sustained loads (rendering, ML, video editing).

Quasar X9 (from $6,499) — Flagship. Top-end CPU and GPU, premium AIO cooling, tempered-glass showcase chassis, optimized for 4K gaming at 120Hz+ and the most demanding workloads.

All three are starting points. The configurator lets you adjust within any tier.
What about returns?
We test every machine before it ships, so it leaves working. Because of that, the only returns we accept are for systems damaged in transit — if your rig arrives damaged, contact us within 48 hours and we'll make it right (this is exactly what optional shipping insurance is for). Before parts are ordered, you can cancel for a full refund. See the Refunds & cancellations section in Policies below for the full text.
I have a question that isn't here.
Use the Request a Build form above and put your question in the notes field. Or email us directly at voidforgesystems@gmail.com — a real human reads everything that comes in.

Refunds & cancellations
Draft

Before parts are ordered (during the quote stage): full refund, no questions.

After parts are ordered: the parts have been purchased and labor is committed for your specific build, so we can't simply cancel for a full refund — but reach out and we'll work out what's fair based on what's already been bought and done.

Shipping & transit damage
Draft

All builds ship via ground freight (UPS or FedEx), carefully packed for transit. Tracking is provided. Most US destinations arrive within 5 business days of dispatch. Optional shipping insurance is available at checkout — we recommend it so a damaged delivery is fully covered.

Transit damage: inspect your package before signing. Photograph any visible exterior damage. If your system is damaged in transit, contact us within 48 hours of delivery. If you added shipping insurance, the claim covers a repair or replacement and we'll help you file it and make it right.

We do not currently ship outside the continental United States.
